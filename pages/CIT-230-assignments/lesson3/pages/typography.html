Brand Message

The message this site seeks to exude is one of authority, trust, style, modern, and slim.

For this reason the first of the font pairing will be a serif font, slim, modern, easy to read, not thick or dense.

Font Pairing: Lora & Nunito

Font: Lora

Lora was chosen as the primary font to deliver a feeling of authority while remaining light and slim.

According to Google Fonts, the font Lora, designed by Cyreal, "is a well-balanced contemporary serif with roots in calligraphy. It is a text typeface with moderate contrast well suited for body text. A paragraph set in Lora will make a memorable appearance because of its brushed curves in contrast with driving serifs. The overall typographic voice of Lora perfectly conveys the mood of a modern-day story, or an art essay. Technically Lora is optimised for screen appearance, and works equally well in print.

Font Nunito

Nunito was selected as the body font in the pairing because it is similar to the Lora font with its slim and light features but without the flares and flags seen on a serif font such as Lora.

According to Google Fonts, the font Lora, designed by Vernon Adams, "Nunito is a well balanced sans serif typeface superfamily, with 2 versions: The project began with Nunito, created by Vernon Adams as a rounded terminal sans serif for display typography. Jacques Le Bailly extended it to a full set of weights, and an accompanying regular non-rounded terminal version, Nunito Sans.

Why These fonts make a great pair

These two fonts compliment one another as a pair and bolster the brand message of authoritative, sleek, and modern. Their type set features are similar to one another well enough that one feels as an extension of the other. The transition of flamboyant serif shapes to a more streamlined body font feels natural. Both fonts feel like brother and sister from the same creator.
